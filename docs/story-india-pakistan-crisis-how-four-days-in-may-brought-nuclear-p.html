India-Pakistan Crisis: How Four Days in May Brought Nuclear Powers to the Brink

A detailed reconstruction of the May 2025 India-Pakistan standoff reveals just how close the two nuclear-armed nations came to armed conflict.

A new analysis from the Stimson Center reconstructs the tense four days in May 2025 when India and Pakistan found themselves on the brink of military conflict following a terrorist attack in Indian-administered Kashmir.

The crisis began when militants attacked an Indian military convoy, killing 14 soldiers. India quickly attributed the attack to Pakistan-based groups and mobilized forces along the Line of Control. Pakistan responded with its own military deployments and raised its nuclear alert status.

Behind-the-scenes diplomacy by China, the United States, and the UAE helped de-escalate the situation over 96 hours. The report reveals previously unknown back-channel communications that were critical in preventing escalation.

"This was the closest the two nations have come to conflict since 2019, and arguably since Kargil," the report states. "The speed of escalation in the social media age makes traditional diplomatic timelines dangerously slow."
